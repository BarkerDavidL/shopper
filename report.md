1. **1970 Datsun 240Z**  
   The Datsun 240Z is widely celebrated for its iconic and sporty design. With a robust 2.4L inline-six engine, it delivers impressive acceleration and agile handling, providing an exhilarating driving experience. Reliability is a strong point, making it appealing to classic car enthusiasts, while its relatively low purchase price encourages entry into classic car ownership. However, potential buyers should note the limited cabin space and susceptibility to rust, alongside basic safety features that don't meet modern standards. Overall, it offers a balance of performance, style, and reliability, making it a top choice.

2. **1973 Triumph TR6**  
   The Triumph TR6 features a powerful 2.5L inline-six engine, delivering strong performance and an engaging driving experience. Its distinctive and muscular design draws significant attention from classic car enthusiasts, while good handling and a supportive community enhance the ownership experience. However, its interior lacks modern comforts, and fuel efficiency is not competitive by today's standards. The TR6 also suffers from rust issues and basic safety concerns. Overall, it is a thrilling classic car that appeals to those willing to engage with its maintenance challenges.

3. **1970 Fiat 124 Sport Spider**  
   The Fiat 124 Sport Spider boasts a stylish and timeless design, offering an engaging driving experience with its agile handling and convertible top for pleasurable open-air driving. It features a decent 1.4L engine which balances power and efficiency for spirited outings. However, limitations such as cramped cabin space and historical rust concerns may impact buyer interest. Additionally, it lacks modern safety features, presenting challenges for safety-conscious consumers. It remains an attractive option for those seeking an affordable classic.

4. **1972 MG Midget**  
   The MG Midget remains a beloved classic known for its lightweight and agile handling, providing a spirited driving experience from its peppy 1.1L engine. The design is appealing to enthusiasts, and its low maintenance costs contribute to its value. However, limited comfort and performance constraints when compared to modern vehicles, along with rust susceptibility and a lack of safety features, could deter some potential buyers. Despite these downsides, its charm and historical significance make it an inviting option for classic car ownership.

Overall, the ranking reflects a careful consideration of performance, comfort, reliability, and historical significance, leading to the Datsun 240Z topping the list for its balance of qualities, while the MG Midget occupies the last position for its significant limitations despite its charm.
